Mango (Mangifera indica)

Basic Information

Scientific Name
Mangifera indica
Common Name(s)
Mango
Botanical Family
Anacardiaceae

Origin and Distribution

Native Region(s)
South Asia Native to the Indian subcontinent, including India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.
Current Distribution
Widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions around the world, including Africa, the Americas, Southeast Asia, and Australia. It is also grown in many subtropical areas in temperate regions.

Physical Characteristics

Leaves

Shape and Size
The leaves are lance-shaped or elliptical, typically 15-35 cm long and 6-16 cm wide. They are alternately arranged along the stem.
Color and Texture
The leaves are lance-shaped or elliptical, typically 15-35 cm long and 6-16 cm wide. They are alternately arranged along the stem.
Arrangement on Stem
Leaves are arranged alternately on the stem.

Flowers Information

Flower

Flower Structure
The flowers are small, with five petals, and are arranged in large, loose clusters called panicles. Each flower has a small, tubular shape.
Color and Size
Flowers are typically white to pale yellow or pink. Each flower is about 5 mm in diameter.
Blooming Season
Mango trees usually bloom in spring or early summer, depending on the region. In tropical climates, flowering can occur year-round.

Fruit Information

Fruit

Type
Drupe (a fleshy fruit with a single seed enclosed in a hard shell).
Size-Shape
The fruit is typically oval, round, or kidney-shaped, and varies in size from 10-20 cm long. It has a thick, leathery skin that can be green, yellow, red, or a combination of these colors when ripe.
Edibility
The fruit is edible and highly valued for its sweet, juicy flesh. It is commonly eaten fresh, dried, or processed into juices, jams, and desserts.

Ecological Information

Pollination

Pollination Mechanism
The flowers are pollinated by insects, including bees, butterflies, and moths.
Pollinators Involved
Insect pollinators are crucial for fruit set, although some mango varieties can set fruit through wind pollination as well.

Medicinal Value

Traditional Uses
: Various parts of the mango tree have been used in traditional medicine. Mango leaves and bark have been used to treat ailments such as diabetes, digestive issues, and infections.
Active Compounds
Various parts of the mango tree have been used in traditional medicine. Mango leaves and bark have been used to treat ailments such as diabetes, digestive issues, and infections.
Modern Medicinal Applications
Research suggests that mangoes may help with digestive health, skin health, and immune function. The fruit is rich in vitamins and antioxidants, which contribute to overall health and wellness.

Cultivation

Soil Requirements
Prefers well-drained, sandy loam soils. Mangoes can tolerate a range of soil types but do best in soils with good drainage.
Water Needs
Requires regular watering, especially during dry periods. However, it is relatively drought-tolerant once established. Overwatering should be avoided to prevent root rot.
Light Requirements
Thrives in full sun, requiring at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
Propagation Methods
Typically propagated from seeds, grafting, or budding. Grafting and budding are preferred for maintaining specific varieties and ensuring consistent fruit quality.
Common Pests and Diseases
Susceptible to pests such as fruit flies, mango weevils, and scale insects. Regular monitoring and appropriate pest control measures are necessary. Can be affected by diseases such as anthracnose (a fungal infection), powdery mildew, and bacterial black spot. Proper care and disease management practices are essential for healthy trees.

Description

General Description
The mango tree is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height. It has a broad, dense canopy with dark green, glossy leaves. The tree is known for its attractive, fragrant flowers and its large, flavorful fruits.
Habitat
Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is commonly found in orchards, home gardens, and commercial plantations. Mango trees prefer sunny locations and well-drained soils.
Growth Habit
Mango trees have a robust and spreading growth habit. They are fast-growing and develop a large, rounded canopy. The tree is often used as an ornamental tree in addition to its agricultural value for fruit production.
